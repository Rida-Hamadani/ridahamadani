The Harvest

I didn't reap what I sow
I sang to the seed in the plough
What I watered didn't grow
How dare the Earth break its vow?

I kept every promise I know
Yet the river refused to go
Worms too lazy to burrow
Dirt cold in the snow

Away ran the scarecrow
Chased by a small crow
And the well drank in one draught
All I saved for the drought

The sky spoke to the earth below
With every tide and undertow
Of how I fell so low
And how I reap my own woe
